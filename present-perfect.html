Present Perfect

Pretérito Perfecto

The present perfect describes past actions or experiences that have a connection to the present. It is formed with the present tense of haber + a past participle.

When to Use It Key uses

Recent past actions

Actions that happened recently, often today or this week.

→ "He comido pizza hoy." (I have eaten pizza today.)

Life experiences

Things you have (or have never) done in your life, with no specific time.

→ "Nunca he visto esa película." (I have never seen that film.)

Repeated past actions

Actions that have happened multiple times up to now.

→ "He viajado a España tres veces." (I have traveled to Spain three times.)

Actions with present result

A past action whose result is still felt or relevant now.

→ "She has broken her leg." → "Se ha roto la pierna."

Time Markers Clue words

hoy (today) esta semana (this week) este mes (this month) este año (this year) ya (already) todavía no (not yet) alguna vez (ever) nunca (never) siempre (always) recientemente (recently) últimamente (lately) esta mañana (this morning)

Examples In context

  • Yo he comido tacos hoy.I have eaten tacos today.
  • ¿Tú has visto esa película?Have you seen that movie?
  • Ella ha escrito tres cartas esta semana.She has written three letters this week.
  • Nosotros hemos viajado a España dos veces.We have traveled to Spain twice.
  • Ellos han hecho la tarea.They have done the homework.
  • Nunca he probado el ceviche.I have never tried ceviche.
